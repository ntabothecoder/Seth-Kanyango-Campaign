Seth Kanyango's Vision

A Five-Point Manifesto for KUPPET Organizing Secretary

1. Empowering Members Through Accessible Resources and Training

We will establish a robust online portal and organize regular workshops to provide KUPPET members with comprehensive information on their rights, benefits, and professional development opportunities. This includes legal aid resources, financial literacy programs, and pedagogical training to enhance teaching skills and career progression. Our goal is to ensure every member is well-equipped and confident in their professional journey.

2. Strengthening Collective Bargaining and Welfare

I commit to vigorously advocating for improved terms and conditions of service for all educators. This includes pushing for better remuneration, timely promotions, adequate health insurance, and favorable working environments. We will engage in transparent and effective negotiations with relevant stakeholders to secure tangible benefits that directly impact the welfare and morale of our members.

3. Enhancing Communication and Transparency within KUPPET

A strong union thrives on open communication. I pledge to foster a culture of transparency by establishing clear and accessible channels for feedback, queries, and grievances. Regular digital newsletters, town hall meetings (both virtual and physical), and a dedicated hotline will ensure that members are always informed, heard, and actively participate in union decisions and activities.

4. Promoting Professional Growth and Recognition

I will champion initiatives that recognize the invaluable contributions of educators and promote their continuous professional growth. This includes advocating for structured mentorship programs, facilitating access to higher education and specialized certifications, and lobbying for policies that reward merit and dedication. Every KUPPET member's professional journey should be one of continuous learning and well-deserved recognition.

5. Building Unity and Solidarity Across All Chapters

My vision is a unified KUPPET, where solidarity transcends geographical and professional boundaries. I will work tirelessly to bridge communication gaps between different chapters, organize inter-county events, and foster a strong sense of community among members. By standing together, we amplify our voice and achieve greater impact in advocating for the rights and welfare of all Kenyan teachers.
